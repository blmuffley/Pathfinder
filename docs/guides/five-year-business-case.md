# Avennorth Pathfinder — Five-Year Business Case

## Executive Summary

Avennorth Pathfinder is a 4-product platform for CMDB-first integration discovery, AI-powered intelligence, autonomous CMDB governance, and service map analytics — built for ServiceNow environments.

**The thesis:** Pathfinder solves the single biggest CMDB problem every ServiceNow customer has — unknown, undocumented, stale integrations — and once deployed it becomes operationally impossible to remove. This creates a binary outcome: rapid adoption to dominant market share, or ServiceNow acquires us to fill the gap in their platform. Both are wins.

**Why it spreads fast:** $50K entry is trivial for any enterprise SN customer. Proves value in weeks, not months. No alternative exists natively in ServiceNow. Compass partner distribution amplifies without a sales team. Word-of-mouth in the tight SN ecosystem is explosive once 30-50 customers validate.

**Why it sticks:** Pathfinder agents become infrastructure. CMDB data integrity depends on continuous observation. Workflows, dashboards, and analyst habits are built on top. Removing Pathfinder means your CMDB degrades back to stale spreadsheets within 90 days. Logo churn will be structurally low.

**Likely case:** ~$84M Pathfinder ARR by Year 5 on ~16 FTEs. $5.25M ARR/employee. 95% gross margin. $89M ending cash.

**Exit optionality:** At $84M ARR with 546 ServiceNow customers, Pathfinder is a strategic acquisition target for ServiceNow at 15-25x ARR ($1.3B-$2.1B). If they don't buy us, we own the category.

## 2. Why This Wins

### 2.1 The Pain Point is Universal and Acute

Every ServiceNow customer with a CMDB has this problem:

| Reality | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| 40-70% of integrations are undocumented | CAB decisions made on incomplete data |
| Integration documentation goes stale in weeks | Every reorg, cloud migration, or app deploy creates drift |
| Manual mapping takes 6-12 months for a large enterprise | By the time you finish, the first half is already wrong |
| SN Discovery maps infrastructure, not integration relationships | The gap between "what servers exist" and "how they talk" is unaddressed |
| EA relationship records don't match runtime reality | Enterprise Architecture is fiction without continuous validation |

**No one else solves this natively in ServiceNow.** Datadog/Dynatrace are APM tools — they watch application performance, not integration relationships. ServiceNow's own Service Mapping is pattern-based and doesn't observe actual network behavior. There is no direct competitor for CMDB-native integration discovery with AI intelligence.

### 2.2 Time to Value is Measured in Days, Not Months

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| Agent deployed on first 20 servers | Day 1-2 |
| First integrations discovered and classified | Hour 1 |
| Full production estate discovered | Week 2-4 |
| AI health scores populated | Day 1 (with agent data) |
| CMDB Ops cleaning up stale records | Week 1 |
| Coverage gaps identified with remediation plan | Week 1 |

The customer sees a live integration map with confidence scores **the same day** agents are deployed. This is not a 6-month implementation — it's a 6-hour revelation. The "aha moment" happens in the first demo.

### 2.3 Adoption Dynamics

**Why it spreads fast in the ServiceNow ecosystem:**

1. **$50K is below the procurement threshold** for most enterprises. A VP can approve it without a committee.
2. **Compass partners are economically incentivized** — 20-30% markup on every license, recurring.
3. **Word-of-mouth is concentrated** — ServiceNow user groups, Knowledge conference, and partner communities are a tight network. 30-50 validated customers creates a tipping point.
4. **Every CoreX consulting engagement becomes a demo** — consultants use Pathfinder during implementations, clients see the value live, purchase follows.
5. **The upgrade trigger is built into the product** — Standard surfaces gaps that only Professional can address. The sales conversation writes itself.

## 3. Switching Costs & Stickiness

### 3.1 Why Customers Cannot Remove Pathfinder

Once deployed, Pathfinder creates **seven layers of dependency** that make removal operationally destructive:

| Layer | What Happens If Removed | Impact |
|-------|------------------------|--------|
| **Agent infrastructure** | Agents on every server stop reporting. Coverage gaps immediately appear across the fleet. | Operational blind spot in <24 hours |
| **CMDB data freshness** | Integration CIs stop receiving health updates. Confidence scores go stale. Within 90 days, the CMDB is back to its pre-Pathfinder state. | CMDB degrades to stale spreadsheet |
| **Workflow dependencies** | Coverage gap remediation flows, health alerting, EA reconciliation — all break. | IT operations lose automated governance |
| **Analyst habits** | Integration analysts use the Polaris workspace daily. Removing it means retraining on manual processes. | Productivity drops immediately |
| **Institutional knowledge** | AI-generated summaries, health baselines, and classification history are lost. This represents months of accumulated intelligence. | Organizational memory erased |
| **Audit & compliance** | Governance field enforcement, compliance checking, and remediation audit trails disappear. | Compliance gaps re-emerge |
| **Cross-product dependencies** | Bearing consumes Pathfinder's confidence feed. Removing Pathfinder breaks Bearing's assessment accuracy. | Portfolio cascade failure |

### 3.2 Churn Modeling Reflects Stickiness

Pathfinder is **infrastructure software**, not a SaaS tool. It is more analogous to Datadog or Splunk (embedded in operations, removal causes data loss) than to a point solution.

| Metric | Typical B2B SaaS | Infrastructure SaaS | Pathfinder (Modeled) |
|--------|-----------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Annual logo churn | 10-15% | 3-7% | 3-6% |
| NRR | 110-130% | 120-150% | 145-165% |
| Time to full dependency | 6-12 months | 1-3 months | 2-4 weeks |
| Removal difficulty | Cancel subscription | Major migration project | Operationally destructive |

**Revised churn assumptions** (reflecting stickiness):

| Metric | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 | Year 4 | Year 5 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Logo churn (Likely) | 3% | 5% | 5% | 6% | 6% |
| Logo churn (Bear) | 5% | 8% | 8% | 8% | 8% |

These rates are lower than previously modeled because the switching cost analysis supports them. Customers who deploy Pathfinder and see value in week 1 are not leaving.

### 4.1 Why ServiceNow Would Acquire Pathfinder

ServiceNow has a pattern of acquiring companies that fill platform gaps:

| Acquisition | Year | Gap Filled | Price |

| Lightstep | 2023 | Observability / distributed tracing | ~$500M |

**Pathfinder fills a known gap:** ServiceNow's Service Mapping is pattern-based. It does not observe actual network behavior. Integration CIs are not a first-class concept in the SN data model. Pathfinder's CMDB-native approach (extends cmdb_ci, Polaris workspace, Flow Designer integration) is designed to be acquired — it slots into the platform as if ServiceNow built it.

### 4.4 The Binary Outcome

**Scenario A — ServiceNow acquires:** Strategic premium at 15-25x ARR. Likely Year 3-5. Founders and team join SN's ITOM division. Pathfinder becomes "ServiceNow Integration Intelligence."

**Scenario B — ServiceNow doesn't acquire:** Pathfinder owns the category. $84M+ ARR growing 80%+ YoY. 91.6% EBITDA margin. $89M cash position. IPO-ready or extremely attractive to PE.

**Both outcomes are wins.** The only losing scenario is if the product doesn't work — and we've already built it, tested it, and know the pain point is universal.

### 10.1 Three Structural Advantages

1. **Existing team leverage.** 3 Avennorth team members contribute to Pathfinder at no incremental cost. The $420K annual allocation is already budgeted in the consulting practice.

2. **Claude-assisted development.** Two engineers + Claude Code delivered what traditionally requires 5-7 engineers. 10 phases, 4 products, 104+ tests, 4 languages (Go, Python, C/eBPF, JS/SN). This multiplier persists.

3. **Compass as distribution engine.** Zero-cost distribution through ServiceNow consulting firms. $8-15K CAC vs. $35-50K direct. 2-4 week SOW line item vs. 6-month enterprise sales cycle.

### 10.2 Intelligence is the Moat

Discovery is table stakes. The moat is the intelligence layer:
- **AI-powered health scoring** — 4-metric weighted composite. No competitor does this natively.
- **Autonomous CMDB agents** — 8 agents with observe/diagnose/recommend/act/verify lifecycle.
- **Change impact analysis** — BFS graph traversal for blast radius. Integrates into CAB.
- **EA reconciliation** — 3-strategy matching. Automates a manual spreadsheet exercise.

These features only produce value when fed by Pathfinder's discovery data, creating a **flywheel that deepens the moat** with every deployment.
